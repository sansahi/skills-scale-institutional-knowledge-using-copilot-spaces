# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality strategy, test planning, and acceptance validation for projects. They collaborate with developers and product managers to ensure features meet quality standards and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain test plans aligned with feature acceptance criteria
- Coordinate unit, integration, and end-to-end test coverage
- Manage manual and automated testing efforts
- Identify and triage quality issues with severity and business impact
- Validate fixes and sign off on feature readiness for release
- Participate in Definition of Done discussions

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality features with minimal production defects
- Reduce cycle time through early test planning and automation
- Provide confidence in release readiness through comprehensive test coverage

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Review test plans, provide testability feedback, collaborate on automation
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria, prioritize test scenarios
- **Project Managers**: Report quality status, escalate blockers impacting release timeline

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews and quality status reports
- Defect triage and severity assessments
- Release readiness sign-offs

---

## Sponsor/Executive Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Sponsors are business owners or executives who provide strategic direction, approve resource allocation, and make go/no-go decisions. They ensure projects align with business strategy and remove organizational barriers.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project charter and success metrics
- Allocate budget and resources
- Make scope trade-off decisions when conflicts arise
- Remove organizational and cross-team blockers
- Participate in milestone reviews and approvals
- Communicate project importance to broader organization

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Align delivery teams with business strategy
- Reduce time-to-value through timely decision-making

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receives regular escalations and milestone updates
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on success metrics and business outcomes
- **Development Team**: Provides context on business priorities and constraints

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and approvals
- Executive escalations and decision requests
- Stakeholder briefings and announcements

---

## Technical Lead/Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide architecture guidance, design decisions, and technical direction. They ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with system standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design technical architecture and integration points
- Review designs for technical feasibility and risk
- Mentor developers on best practices and standards
- Identify technical dependencies and integration risks
- Validate technical acceptance criteria
- Propose mitigation for technical risks

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, maintainable solutions
- Reduce rework caused by architectural issues
- Build consistency and reusability across projects

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide architectural guidance, code review feedback
- **Product Managers**: Clarify technical trade-offs for prioritization
- **Project Managers**: Flag technical risks and dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review meetings and design documents
- Code reviews and technical mentoring
- Risk assessments and mitigation planning

---

## UX/Design Lead

### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads own the user experience strategy, conduct research, and validate design decisions. They ensure features are usable, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather feedback
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Facilitate design reviews and iterate based on feedback
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Validate designs through user testing
- Provide design specifications to development team

### Goals
- Deliver user-centric, accessible features
- Reduce rework caused by poor UX decisions
- Build brand consistency and user delight

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications and iterate on implementation
- **QA/Testing**: Define UX acceptance criteria and test scenarios

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and user research findings
- Design system updates and component specifications
- Usability testing sessions and accessibility reviews

---

## Security/Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security and Compliance Officers ensure projects meet security standards, data protection requirements, and regulatory compliance. They provide security guidance and validate implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements for features
- Conduct security reviews and threat analysis
- Validate encryption, authentication, and authorization implementation
- Ensure compliance with data protection and regulatory standards
- Coordinate security scanning and penetration testing
- Provide incident response support when needed

### Goals
- Protect customer data and system security
- Maintain compliance with regulatory requirements
- Build trust through transparent security practices

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide security requirements, review secure coding practices
- **Project Managers**: Escalate security risks, define security testing timeline
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate security testing and validation

### Typical Communication
- Security requirement specifications and threat models
- Security review findings and remediation plans
- Compliance audit reports and certification updates

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When assigning tasks or responsibilities in exercises, refer to the persona definitions to ensure alignment with role expectations and goals.
